42 Silicon Valley

Wade Hickey's Portfolio
2017-03-19

Sherlock: Poetry or truth?
Gregson: Many would say they’re the same thing.
Sherlock: Yes, idiots.

Truth:

What is 42?
Aggregated autodidactic fun centered around programming.
An antithesis to the archetypal university’s structure.

How do I get in?
Have a good work ethic and an IQ above 120.
Take the online test to prove such. Schedule a piscine.
Work hard through the piscine. Spend more than 10 hours a day actively learning.
If you’re intellectually honest, and put forth the required effort, you will be accepted.
Good luck getting a dorm, though.

Is it worth it?
I refuse to pass on binary value judgements.
Make up your own mind.

Poetry:

In an 80’s model dormitory, situated a 4 minute walk away from our programming lab, we slept at odd hours and with unbalanced schedules.
The lab was a dreamscape pulled from the mind of the captain of a spacecraft of some alien species from a place far less novel than 42 proved to be.
42 was a place of contraposition.
We lived in a chaotic vacuum of control, whilst dedicating our time almost monasticly to the practice of controlling abstractions.
We were a high tech collective that never had solid internet access.
We were multi-cultured and poly-lingual but we mostly spoke C.
We were ahead of our time and our predictive capabilities were nullified.
In an environment sold to the world (and my parents) as a quick way to learn a trade and become employed, most of us didn’t see programming as work, but as time free to explore a relatively new modal of creative explication.

The pedagogy of peer to peer teaching/learning is tied to a long standing tradition originating in schools the co-founder Kwame helped birth.
Similarly, most of the staff were also students from the 42 ecole of France, which helped to construct a self-improving feedback loop.
The idea of tuition-free universities were expanding in the cultural zeitgeist, much like the mycelium-landscape of silicon valley, only Fremont was a much better substrate and we outclassed our international peers.
Despite our superiority, The (French) staff had an idea as to what they were doing when they bought the campus outright.
They let Devry continue it’s lease during construction, almost as if to shame them, to beat the ideological dead horse.
They set up our lab in the dying bones of the for-profit school except we were open 24/7 and tuition was free.
It was a belittling nod to the typical university macrocosm: the years of internment, temporary rote memorization, and crippling student debt.
And yet, suddenly it seemed as though that macrocosm was approaching its death spiral.
In a culture of creativity stifling structure, self-directed learning was 42’s solution, and with a Bruce-Waynesque billionaire backer, It was promising.

Problems to contemplate:

It seems like a glorified hostel with publicly available learning materials.
No degree. No intention of accredidation. No proof of success in the US, opportunity cost, etc.
Really, in 2017 why doesn’t the internet work? Like it’s just some wires and electricity, come on…
The website is full of grammatical and typographical errors.
The problem with revolutions is that they end where they began.

Response to contemplated problems to contemplate:

42 is not a building, or a bunch of computers, or blog posts, or marketing slogans.
42 is not 10 million dollars in funding, or a tightly woven sound-bite from a few industry professionals.
42 is not words.
42 is a platform by which like-minded individuals can solve problems,
ultimately to learn to program
(ultimately to solve larger problems (ad infinitum)).

Conclusion

I couldn’t sculpt a better environment, the void of cultural cohesion, the anti-pattern in a sea of grey.
42 treated us as agents without any factory structured directionality, we were unbounded, free.
For that, 42 is unique.
My interest in programming, as an end in itself, has waned and become just another useful methodology to add to the analogical toolbox.
During my time at 42, I’ve become less a student/teacher so much as a thoughtful spectator trying to best conceptualize the chaos.
